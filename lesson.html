Attributes of Categorical Propositions Categorical propositions have three 

important attributes: quality, quantity, and distribution. Each of these attributes helps us understand how propositions work.QualityQuality refers to whether
 a proposition affirms or denies something about a category.Affirmative
 Propositions: These statements affirm a relationship. For example, “All roses are flowers” indicates that roses belong to the group of flowers
.
Negative Propositions: These statements deny a relationship. For instance, “No cats are reptiles” means that cats do not belong to the category of reptiles. Quantity Quantity

tells us how many members of a category the proposition refers to. Universal Quantity: This type of statement covers all members of a category.For example, “All birds are animals” means every bird is included in the category of animals. Particular Quantity: This type of statement refers to some members of a category. For example, “Some birds can swim” indicates that there are birds that can swim, but not all birds can do so. Distribution Distribution indicates whether a term refers to all members of a category or just some. Distributed Terms: In “All dogs are mammals,” the term “dogs” refers to every dog, so it is fully distributed. Undistributed Terms: In “Some dogs are friendly,” the term “dogs” refers only to some dogs and is not fully distributed.
